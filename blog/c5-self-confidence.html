Self Confidence

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

In chapter 4 we learn that mindfulness and self awareness are essentially the same thing! The traditional analogy of this mind is a fluttering flag on a flagpole, where the flag represents the mind. When experiencing strong emotions the mind may be turbulent causing the flag to flutter but with mindfulness this acts as a flagpole, this keeps the mind grounded. Stopping the flag from fluttering away.

Body Scan

When doing a body scan it was nice to take a moment to focus on sensations that I am experiencing throughout my body, from my head all the way down to my toes. Once I took some time to focus on specific areas of the body I noticed much more feeling or sensation was going on then I originally thought. After experiencing these sensations through-out the body I was instructed to think of a nice thought, A time or place in which I felt optimal and productive . I was able to experience what positive emotion felt like in the body and how if effected what sensations that were going on in the body.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

In this section we have a look at how our emotions are simply what we are feeling instead of who we are. With enough mindfulness practice you may notice a subtle but important shift, Emotions go from a being an existential (“I am”) to experiential (“I feel”) experience. The importance of this shift is that it enables the possibility of mastery over ones emotions!
